Florence Nightingale's Coxcomb: Causes of Death (1854–1856)

This is a modernized version of Nightingale's original coxcomb infographic. The interactive chart below shows deaths by various causes over months and years. Data adapted from Nightingale’s original records (1854–1856). Use the play button to animate across years or click the legends to isolate causes.

Florence Nightingale (1820–1910) was a British nurse, reformer, and statistician. Her coxcomb chart was a pioneering example of data storytelling, using visual evidence to drive healthcare reform.
